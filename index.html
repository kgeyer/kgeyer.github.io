I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Mathematics & Statistics at Boston University, and advised by Joshua Campbell and Masanao Yajima at the Campbell Lab. We develop statistical methology for scientific discovery from genomic data, specifically single cell RNA sequencing data.

Before graduate school, I was a staff member at MIT Lincoln Laboratory for four years, where I developed tools for text analytics and signal processing. My current (and former) research interests include,

  • Probabilistic topic modeling with Latent Dirichlet Analysis (LDA) & autoencoders
  • Convex optimization & machine learning
  • Over-parameterization in deep learning & matrix sensing
  • Signal processing & analytics of text data
