I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Mathematics & Statistics at Boston University, and advised by Joshua Campbell and Masanao Yajima. Our research focuses on develop of statistical methology for scientific discovery from genomic data.

Before graduate school, I was a staff member at MIT Lincoln Laboratory for four years, where I developed tools for text analytics and signal processing. My current (and former) research interests include,

  • Topic modeling with Bayesian hierarchical models & autoencoders
  • High dimensional statistics & convex optimization
  • Over-parameterization in deep learning & matrix sensing
  • Signal processing & analytics of unstructured text data
